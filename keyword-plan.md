To dominate the search results for **Clever Creative**, our keyword approach must move beyond generic terms like "graphic design" and focus on **high-intent "money" keywords** and **top-of-funnel authority builders**. 

In the creative industry, we aren't just selling a service; we are selling a transformation. Our keywords should reflect the sophisticated language our ideal clients use when they are ready to invest.

---

### 1. The "Three-Tier" Keyword Strategy
We will categorize our targets into three distinct buckets to ensure we capture users at every stage of the marketing funnel:

* **Tier 1: High-Intent (Bottom of Funnel)**
    * *Goal:* Conversion.
    * *Examples:* "Premium branding agency for tech startups," "Squarespace web design for luxury brands," "Custom visual identity packages."
* **Tier 2: Educational (Middle of Funnel)**
    * *Goal:* Authority & E-E-A-T.
    * *Examples:* "How to rebrand a heritage company," "What to include in a creative brief," "Logo vs. Visual Identity."
* **Tier 3: Trend-Based (Top of Funnel)**
    * *Goal:* High volume/Brand awareness.
    * *Examples:* "2026 Typography Trends," "Minimalist packaging design inspiration."

### 2. Leveraging Long-Tail "Creative" Modifiers
Squarespace sites often perform best when they own a specific niche. Instead of competing for "Web Design," we will use **modifiers** that signal our premium status:
* **Industry Modifiers:** "...for Boutique Hotels," "...for E-commerce," "...for B2B SaaS."
* **Quality Modifiers:** "Bespoke," "Award-winning," "Strategic," "High-end."

### 3. Competitor Gap Analysis
We don't want to copy what other agencies are doing; we want to see what they are **ignoring**. 
* **The Strategy:** Identify "People Also Ask" (PAA) boxes for keywords like "How much does a branding agency cost?" or "ROI of a website redesign." 
* **The Gap:** Most agencies hide their processes. By creating content that answers these "uncomfortable" questions, we build **Trustworthiness (the 'T' in E-E-A-T)** faster than the competition.

### 4. Technical Execution in Squarespace
Squarespace handles technical SEO differently than WordPress. We must map our keywords to specific fields within the interface:
* **Page Titles:** Keep the most important keyword at the **front** of the SEO Title field.
* **URL Slugs:** Keep them short and keyword-rich (e.g., `/branding-agency-los-angeles` instead of `/what-we-do-at-clever-creative`).
* **Image Alt Text:** Since we are a visual agency, every portfolio image is an SEO opportunity. Don't use "Project 1"; use "Minimalist Skincare Packaging Design - Clever Creative."
